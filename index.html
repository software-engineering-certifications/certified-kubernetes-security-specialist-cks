What is Certified Kubernetes Security Specialist (CKS)?

The Certified Kubernetes Security Specialist (CKS) programme verifies that a CKS possesses the necessary skills, knowledge, and competence to secure container-based applications and Kubernetes platforms during development, deployment, and runtime. This exam requires CKA certification to take.

CKS is a performance-based certification exam that assesses candidates' understanding of Kubernetes and cloud security in a realistic, simulated environment.

Benefits

What are the benefits of "Kubernetes" Certification?

Certifications are always important in any job. Some Kubernetes experts may argue that credentials are of little use; yet, this certification demonstrates an individual's ability to use Kubernetes' core software to build complicated searches, reports, and dashboards to get the most out of their data.

In the Kubernetes Enterprise and Kubernetes Cloud Platforms, a Kubernetes Core Certified User may search, use fields, use look-ups, and produce basic statistical reports and dashboards. This credential validates a person's ability to browse and operate the Kubernetes software.

Why we should learn Certified Kubernetes Security Specialist (CKS)?

  • The benefits of Kubernetes- Kubernetes, the first Cloud Native Cloud Foundation (CNCF) initiative and a Google donation, is the fastest growing open-source software project after Linux.
  • Flexibility- Kubernetes is a container orchestration tool that can be used on a variety of different infrastructure and environments. Kubernetes can work with virtually any type of underlying infrastructure -- whether it is a public cloud, private cloud, or an on-premises server.
  • Multi-cloud capability- Kubernetes can host multiple workloads that are spread across multiple clouds. In addition, it can easily scale its environment from one cloud to another. These features mean that Kubernetes lends itself well to the multi-cloud strategies that many businesses are pursuing today.
  • Increased developer productivity-With its declarative constructs and ops-friendly attitude, Kubernetes has revolutionized deployment processes and enabled teams to employ GitOps. Teams can scale and deploy at a far faster rate than in the past. Teams can now deploy numerous times per day rather than once per month.
  • Market leader- Kubernetes is gaining traction in enterprise IT environments and is no longer solely a developer community effort. According to a recent survey, 59 percent of respondents use Kubernetes in production.
  • Proven and battle-tested- It would have been risky to use Kubernetes in production four or five years ago. It was a relatively young orchestrator at the time, with few verified production installations.
